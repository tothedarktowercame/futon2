The analogical surface: quantities that wear an Active-Inference name but are heuristics. Companion to aif-wiring-explainer.html (the faithful loop) · source of truth: data/r18-badges.edn (E-r18-faithfulness-audit). Drafted 2026-07-08 (claude-5).

Why a separate page. The main wiring explainer describes the loop that is faithful Active Inference. This page quarantines the parts that are not — the quantities the R18 faithfulness audit badged analogical ("a heuristic wearing the name"). Keeping them here lets the main explainer's labels stay trustworthy, and gives us one honest place to decide, later, which of these actually need resolving. It is a short list — seven items.

For contrast — the FEP-faithful core is only three legs: G = risk + ambiguity − EIG — :kl risk (principled approximation) + :gaussian-entropy ambiguity (the one derived-from-FEP quantity) − the pattern-grain EIG leg (epistemic value, sought). As of 2026-07-08 all three are armed/faithful. Everything below is augmentation — real controller behaviour, but not Expected Free Energy.

What the list says, honestly

The headline unfaithfulness is :G-total itself — the score is labelled "Expected Free Energy" but is a hand-weighted blend of eight incommensurate terms. Faithful EFE is the three-leg core; the blend is a multi-objective controller with an EFE core inside it. That is a real thing to be — it just is not what the label claims.

Two of these are dead or nearly so. :G-info has a 0% flip-rate — it never changes a decision — and it duplicated the epistemic role that the new pattern-grain EIG now fills properly. It is a clean delete. :G-gap is a small caller-supplied lookup with no information-theoretic content.

The load-bearing ones are real controllers wearing the wrong name. :G-survival (47.9% flip) and :G-structural-pressure (44.5%) and :G-graph-pragmatic genuinely drive the War Machine's choices — but as homeostatic / habit / feasibility heuristics, not as Expected Free Energy. The honest repair for these is almost never "delete"; it is "rename to what it is" (exogenous weight, homeostatic pressure) or "move to its canonical AIF seat" (the habit prior ln E(π), a feasibility mask).

Do we need to resolve them? Open question, deliberately deferred. Faithfulness does not require deleting the augmentation — a multi-objective controller with an honest EFE core is a legitimate design. The minimum honest move is truth-in-labelling: stop calling the blend "EFE," and badge each term for what it is (which r18-badges.edn already does). Beyond that: prune :G-info (free — it does nothing), and the canonical-seat repairs are worthwhile but not urgent. Whether the WM should become a purer EFE agent, or stay an honestly-labelled multi-objective one, is a design decision for later.
