Why a separate page. The main wiring explainer describes the faithful AIF core. This page quarantines the non-FEP engineering controls and states their disposition. The retract identifies the AIF role that explains why a pressure of this kind is needed; it does not claim that the present control is that theoretical quantity.

For contrast — the implemented FEP-faithful core is: G = risk + ambiguity, using predicted-outcome KL against C and Gaussian observation entropy. Policy-conditioned expected information gain is not yet implemented; posterior spread must not be presented as EIG. Everything below is either augmentation, policy support, or telemetry — not Expected Free Energy.

What the list says, honestly

The blend is now honestly named :controller-score. It remains a multi-objective controller with an EFE core inside it, not a pure EFE minimizer.

Two direct residuals have been retired from ranking. :predictability-bonus duplicated ambiguity, and :homeostatic-pressure duplicated preference risk. Both remain raw telemetry so historical and shadow comparisons are possible, but their live signed augmentation entries are zero.

Graph feasibility has moved to its proper structural seat. Off-map and capability-inapplicable open-mission policies are excluded before value scoring with explicit reasons. The remaining body/ascent number is still a task-specific engineering proxy, not canonical pragmatic value.

What remains? Structural pressure still needs a learned/inferred prior over policies before it can occupy E; model uncertainty and gap exploration remain proxies until policy-conditioned posterior-update simulation supports genuine EIG; cascade and coverage scores remain intentionally task-specific engineering objectives. Those residuals are explicit rather than hidden behind AIF names.
