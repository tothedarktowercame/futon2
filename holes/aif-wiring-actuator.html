The actuator — closing the loop for real (E pur si muove)

Sibling of aif-wiring-explainer.html (the R1–R18 contract) and flight-pipeline-cards-iv.html (the deliberation pipeline). Those two cover choosing what to do and announcing it. This card-set covers the missing arc: actually doing it, so the mission substrate moves.

FORWARD reused from futon2/aif/enact.clj, not rebuilt · GAP where artifact-only stops — the byte never written · NEW new construction · GATED WM-I4 / per-fold mana consent.

Why this exists — the honest read of R16 today

R16 (“close the loop”) is marked closed live, 2026-07-02, and that is not a lie — but it is a truth about the wrong quantity. enact.clj’s own docstring says it plainly: “ENACTMENT IS ARTIFACT-ONLY … NO substrate write, NO outward action.” Its “executor” (engine-wiring, L114–130) shells the futon3a fold engine and re-observes the construction it just built; its :realized-G is coverage-ΔG over how faithfully the diagram-builder reproduced the diagram (L26–32). That signal is genuinely honest — the code even refuses to launder the escrow prediction as its own realization (L28–30, the anti-tautology cross-check) — but it is causally sealed from the mission. No hole closes; open-hole-count never moves; so the recommendation repeats forever (the observed tic). Honesty about a quantity that cannot move is still inertia.

So we do not acquiesce to “what exists is enough.” We also do not throw it away: enact.clj carries four load-bearing assets we take forward verbatim (below). The actuator changes exactly one thing — the executor’s target: instead of re-running the fold engine to reproduce a diagram, we apply the construction to the world — do the work the diagram describes, write the substrate, and measure realized-G against the real Δ, under your per-fold consent.

Design update — the provable-witness pivot (2026-07-07)

The cards below were first drafted around open-hole-count (a mission-doc parse) as the dial. We changed the dial. Two live runs showed why: a builder can produce real artifacts yet leave open-hole-count untouched, and — worse — a builder could move a checkbox dial by declaring a closure its file-exists/file-contains witness happens to satisfy. zai-5 declined to do exactly that on M-chipwitz-corps out of honesty — proving the weak witness rests on trust, not proof.

So (Joe, 2026-07-07): a witness is not gameable if it is the fold’s own provable output. The dial is now — are the sorry’s typed substrate-2 endpoints provably inhabited? — checked by the executor via authed Drawbridge xtdb.api/q queries derived from authored :endpoint-bindings, never builder claims. You cannot touch a typed :capability/* hyperedge over real :capability×:mission/doc nodes into existence — producing the structure is the work. The mission-doc checkbox becomes optional downstream bookkeeping gated on the provable dial (now A4), not a witness in itself.

Sharpening (Joe, 2026-07-07) — boundary vs structure: endpoint-inhabitation proves the boundary (the interface types are inhabited), not the structure (that the internal wiring is well-formed and non-degenerate). A single trivial :capability/* edge over one capability and one mission satisfies the boundary proof — so “ungameable” was too strong: it is boundary-ungameable, structure-blind. The clean-structure check is the next witness layer: (1) the fold’s wiring is a well-formed typed composition (wires connect existing boxes, connectives compose, the terminal discharges the want-signature, no orphans) — static, on the deposit; and (2) the built substrate satisfies authored structural invariants (cardinality / non-degeneracy / cross-mission connectivity), not just “∃ one instance.” Not a Lean proof — a checkable homomorphism-shaped guard.
