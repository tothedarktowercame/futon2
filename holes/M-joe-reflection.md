## HEAD (Joe, 2026-07-12, verbatim charter)

We have been increasingly close to building the futonzero-alphazero
featureset — and that's great **for FUTON**: learning, adding patterns,
shipping code. **We're missing what's-in-it-for-me.** The dashboard and the
turn record make it "look like" the only thing the operator does is creative
work — building FUTON, directing agents, adding LOC — which is good for what
it is. But the Peircean/Batesonian move is to look at how that work acts on
*the operator*: the signs the operator emits change the system, and the habit
of emitting them changes the operator. This mission gives that second
direction a first-class account. Hence the capability stars; hence
M-futonzero-capability; hence Q5.

The FutonZero frame states it exactly: the reward the stack plays for is the
**peradam — a certified unit of earned capability**. The system's ledger of
earned capability is increasingly real. **The operator has no such ledger.**
What-is-in-it-for-me = the operator's peradam account, at coarse grain,
honestly witnessed.

## Constraint (binding, from Joe's standing position): NO LIFE LOGGING

The observation stream is what already exists — the operator is a *prolific
writer*, and the turns he enters are the corpus (plus marks at one keystroke,
plus witnesses already in stores: commits, sends, snapshots, star-maps).
This mission adds **zero new logging burden**; it reads what is already
written. (The §E terminals in the July review are opt-in ergonomics, not
prerequisites. The witness-inversion rule from the R-audit governs: inner-
lane quantities are narrations, marked as such — R18 faithfulness, not fake
externality.)
